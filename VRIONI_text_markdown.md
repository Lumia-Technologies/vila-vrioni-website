# The Vrioni Family of Berat and Izedin Vrioni, Mayor of Berat: A Biographical and Historical Report

## TL;DR
- **Izedin (Yzedin) Vrioni was the son of Galip Pasha Vrioni of the Fier branch of the Vrioni family and the brother of the politician Qemal bej Vrioni; he is documented as Mayor (Kryetar i Bashkisë) of Berat in 1940 and, according to family and encyclopedic accounts, died in 1947 — English Wikipedia states he was "beaten to death in the local police station," with his remains never recovered.**
- The Vrioni (Ottoman Turkish *Viryon-zâde*) were one of Albania's greatest landowning bey/pasha families, rooted around Berat from the mid-17th century, who produced an Ottoman general (Omer Vrioni), a vice-president of the League of Prizren (Mehmet Ali Pasha Vrioni), an Albanian prime minister and Independence signatory (Iliaz Vrioni), and the translator Jusuf Vrioni.
- After the 1944 communist takeover the family was branded "class enemies": their estates and the historic Berat sarays were nationalized and largely destroyed (1947), and family members were executed, tortured to death, imprisoned, or interned — including Izedin (1947), Sami (died under torture 1947), Qemal (died in prison c. 1950–52), and Jusuf (15 years).

## Key Findings

**On Izedin specifically.** There were two similarly named figures, and sources conflict, but the weight of evidence identifies the Berat mayor as Izedin (also spelled Yzedin), son of **Galip Pasha Vrioni** (Fier branch) and **Nasip hanëm Vlora**, and brother of **Qemal bej Vrioni** (1884/85–1952). This is established by: (1) the Albanian Wikipedia article on Qemal, which states Qemal was born "son of Galip Pasha Vrioni and Nasip hanëm Vlora, among four sisters and his brother, Izedin"; (2) an Albanian state-archive confiscation file (AQSH, F.505, V.1946, D.142) that treats Izedin and Qemal jointly as co-owners of the paternal estate in Fier and Mbrostar; and (3) an official 2016 Albanian heritage decision naming a protected Berat building "objekti i Yzedin Galip Pashë Vrioni." A competing genealogy on fieri.com lists a "Yzedin" among the children of Sami bej Vrioni, but that article is internally inconsistent (it lists overlapping children for both Sami and Galip) and is best treated as an error.

**Mayoral service.** Izedin Vrioni is confirmed as Mayor of Berat in the pre-war Albanian yearbook/census of 1940, which lists for Berat Prefecture: "Prefekt: Avni Koka; Shef i Policisë: Skënder Selmanaj; Kryetar i Bashkisë: Izedin Vrioni; Sekretar: Anastas Goxhahani." The family tradition that he served as mayor several times is plausible but could not be corroborated in available online sources, which document only the single 1940 attestation. The exact years and number of his terms remain undocumented online and likely survive only in Berat municipal records or in Marela Guga's monograph *Vrionasit ndryshe* (2005).

**Fate under communism.** Per the AQSH confiscation file, as of the commission decision of 18 November 1946 the sequestration on Izedin's share of property was ordered lifted because he "had not been sentenced by the court," whereas his brother Qemal's pre-1940 property was confiscated outright. Despite this, English Wikipedia and Albanian encyclopedic/press sources report that Izedin Bej Vrioni died in 1947 — English Wikipedia stating he "was beaten to death in the local police station; his remains are still missing."

## Details

### Origins and identity of the Vrioni family
The Vrioni (Albanian *Vrionasit*; Ottoman Turkish *Viryoni / Viryon-zâde*) were among the largest landowning aristocratic families of Albania, known as *konaqe* or *oxhaqe*. According to the orientalist Vexhi Buharaja, the family originated in the village of Virjon (today Ullinjas, near Berat), where Çelë Ibrahimi, eldest son of the family's historic progenitor Islam Bey, built a fountain in 1685 bearing an inscription with the family genealogy. The surname Virjon evolved into Vrion(i). The family later split into two branches: the Berat branch (holding the title *bey*) and the Fier branch (holding the title *pasha*).

Several sources (the French consul Pouqueville; the Epirote chronicler Aravantinos) record a romantic tradition that the Vrioni descended from the Byzantine Palaiologos dynasty; modern scholarship treats this as unsubstantiated romanticization and affirms the family's Albanian (Tosk) ethnic and geographic roots. Documentarily, the Vrioni appear in the Vilayet of Janina around the mid-17th century as *timarli sipahi* (timariot cavalry) in the Berat area, integrated into Ottoman administration. The sanjak and city of Berat were dominated by the Vrioni family in the 19th century. The family's wealth was consolidated by Kahreman Vrioni, who after the Tanzimat reforms became *mütesarrif* of Berat and bought up estates confiscated from the Alltun and Vlora families.

### Notable family members across generations
- **Omer (Ymer) Pasha Vrioni (d. 1828)** — born in the village of Vrioni near Berat; the most famous Ottoman general of the family. Per İlkay Erken's study (Brill, *Those Infidel Greeks*), he became acquainted with Hurşid Pasha "in Egypt during his sixteen-year service under Mehmed Ali Pasha" (including against Napoleon's expeditionary force, 1798–1801). He was treasurer (*hazinedar*) to Ali Pasha of Yanina, then defected to the Porte in September 1820 in exchange for the Pashalik of Berat (Sanjak of Vlorë), becoming sanjak-bey in January 1821. In the Greek War of Independence he defeated and captured Athanasios Diakos at the Battle of Alamana (24 April 1821, ordering Diakos impaled), but his army of approximately 8,000–9,000 men was checked by Odysseas Androutsos and about 120 men at the Battle of Gravia Inn (8 May 1821); he then captured Livadeia and lifted the siege of the Acropolis in Athens before retreating to Ioannina. He co-besieged Missolonghi (1822–23); recalled in 1824; later led 20,000 men in a failed attempt to relieve the Siege of Varna (Russo-Turkish War, 1828). He died at Kütahya in 1828.
- **Mehmet Ali Pasha Vrioni (1842–1895)** — born in Berat, deputy in the first Ottoman parliament and Vice-President of the League of Prizren (1878); collaborator of Abdyl Frashëri, with whom he traveled to Paris, Rome, Vienna and Berlin (1879) to press Albanian demands at the Congress of Berlin. He was father of the future prime minister Iliaz Vrioni.
- **Omer Pasha Vrioni II (1839–1928)** — founder of the city of Fier (1864, with his son Kahreman Pasha); member of the League of Prizren; member of the High Council of the Regency (1921–1922).
- **Aziz Pasha Vrioni (1859–1920)** — born in Berat, son of Hysejn Pasha Vrioni; deputy in the Ottoman parliament for Berat, Albanian Minister of Finance (1913) and later Minister of Agriculture and Public Works; close ally of Ismail Qemali. He is also remembered as a cultured figure/poet of the family.
- **Iliaz (Iljaz/Ilias) bej Vrioni (1882–1932)** — born in Berat, son of Mehmet Ali Pasha and Hysnije Vokopola of the Vokopola family (making him maternally related to Ferid Vokopola, per Robert Elsie, *A Biographical Dictionary of Albanian History*, I.B. Tauris, 2013, p. 475); Mayor of Berat 1908/09–1911; delegate to the Assembly of Vlora and signatory of the Albanian Declaration of Independence (28 November 1912); three times Prime Minister of Albania and five times Foreign Minister; Plenipotentiary Minister in Paris and London. Decorated Grand Officier de la Légion d'honneur. Died in Paris (17 March 1932); father of the translator Jusuf Vrioni.
- **Sami bej Vrioni (1870/76–1947)** — son of Omer Pasha Vrioni II; signatory of the Declaration of Independence (1912); chamberlain to Prince Wied; delegate to the Congress of Durrës (1918); deputy. Died under torture in 1947.
- **Hysen bej Vrioni (1881–1944)** — son of Aziz Pasha; Albanian Minister of Justice and twice Minister of Foreign Affairs in the interwar period; signed the First Pact of Tirana (1926) on behalf of Ahmet Zogu. Per Albanian Wikipedia, "Hysni beu vetë kishte nën zotërim 7500 hektarë në Myzeqenë Jugore" — he personally held 7,500 hectares in southern Myzeqe.
- **Qemal bej Vrioni (1884/85–1952)** — son of Galip Pasha; deputy and minister (Minister of Finance under the Italian occupation, director of the newspaper *Tomorri*); sentenced to life by the 1945 Special Court; died in Burrel prison.
- **Jusuf (Isuf) Vrioni (1916–2001)** — son of Iliaz; the renowned French translator of Ismail Kadare; arrested 1947, sentenced 1950 to 15 years; pursued for 27 years by the Sigurimi.
- **Nyzhet bej Vrioni (1877–1920)** and **Kahreman Pasha Vrioni** — cultural patriots; delegates to the Congresses of Monastir (1908), Trieste (1913), and Elbasan (1909); funded the first Albanian-language school in Berat and the Elbasan Normal School.

### Landholdings
The Vrioni were among Albania's largest latifundists, with *çiftliks* (estates) concentrated around Berat, Fier, and across the Myzeqe plain. The 1946 archival inventory of the holdings linked to Izedin and Qemal alone lists (among 18 items) houses and shops, a 300 m² plot and a palace in Durrës, 62 shops, 2 warehouses, a bakery, an inn, hundreds of plots in Fier, over 1,000 dynym of manor land and pasture, over 1,500 dynym of arable land in Mbrostar, and large pasture, forest and cultivated tracts in Koxharé and Mbyet. The family also owned the "Myzeqeja" flour mill in Fier. Hysen bej Vrioni separately held 7,500 hectares in southern Myzeqe.

### Izedin Vrioni — focused biography
- **Parents:** Galip Pasha Vrioni (Fier branch; before 1912 himself mayor of Fier) and Nasip hanëm Vlora.
- **Siblings:** brother Qemal bej Vrioni (1884/85–1952) and four sisters.
- **Office:** Mayor (Kryetar i Bashkisë) of Berat — confirmed for 1940; family tradition holds he served multiple terms (uncorroborated online).
- **Property:** co-owner with Qemal of the extensive paternal estate in Fier and Mbrostar; an official heritage-protected building in Berat bears his name ("Yzedin Galip Pashë Vrioni").
- **Fate:** the 1946 confiscation commission lifted the sequestration on his share (he had not been court-sentenced), yet he is reported to have died in 1947 — English Wikipedia states he was beaten to death at the local police station and his remains never found.
- **Open gaps:** birth year, wife and children, and the exact years/number of mayoral terms are undocumented in available online sources.

### Communist-era confiscation and persecution
After 1944 the Vrioni were stigmatized in the official "class struggle" narrative as feudal "bloodsuckers" and traitors, and for four decades served as the archetypal class enemy in Albanian school textbooks. The dictatorship expropriated and nationalized all Vrioni property and destroyed much of their heritage, including the historic Berat sarays in 1947 (only the pavilion where the May 1944 Berat Conference was held survives). The confiscation machinery operated through Law No. 40 (14 January 1945), the December 1944 confiscation law, Decree-Law No. 249 (27 May 1946), and the 22 November 1946 nationalization law, executed by local commissions whose explicit aim — per a 17 May 1946 Justice Ministry circular — was to punish political opponents.

Documented fates of family members:
- **Iliaz Vrioni** — his remains were exhumed in 1946 and thrown into the Osum river in Berat; he has no grave.
- **Sami bej Vrioni** — Independence signatory; died under torture in 1947.
- **Izedin Bej Vrioni** — died 1947 (reported beaten to death at the police station; remains missing).
- **Qemal bej Vrioni** — sentenced to life by the Special Court (1945); died in Burrel prison (sources variously give c. 1950–1952).
- **Kahreman Pasha Vrioni** — died in unclear circumstances in 1955.
- **Irfan bej Vrioni** — sentenced to life imprisonment.
- **Jusuf Vrioni** — sentenced to 15 years (1950); later rehabilitated as a translator.
- **Nermin Vrioni** — died homeless, suffering from dementia.
- Others were interned (e.g., Ali Vrioni in Libofshë; Muharem Vrioni in Roskovec), imprisoned, or — even those who fought as partisans (Hamdi Vrioni, killed in the war; Nesim/Neka Vrioni) — persecuted.

After 1990, Vrioni heirs pursued restitution. The family won a judgment at the European Court of Human Rights in **Vrioni and Others v. Albania and Italy** (applications nos. 35720/04 and 42832/06), judgment of 29 September 2009 (Fourth Section), which found violations of Article 6 §1, Article 13 and Article 1 of Protocol No. 1; the applicants were Shahin Vrioni (b. 1925) and Oliver Vrioni (b. 1946), together with the Italian nationals Gherardo and Dario La Francesca, concerning a plot of land measuring 181 m² within the grounds occupied by the Italian Embassy in Tirana. Press reporting indicates the family ultimately received compensation of roughly €1.9 million; claimant Shahin Vrioni died before benefiting.

## Recommendations
1. **To pin down Izedin's mayoral terms and personal details:** consult Marela Guga, *Vrionasit ndryshe* (Erik botime, 2005) and Asllan Sulo's monograph *Iljas Vrioni* (2004); both Berat authors had access to family and local records. The Berat municipal archive and the Arkivi Qendror Shtetëror (AQSH) — especially fund F.505 (confiscations) and Berat prefecture/municipality administrative files — are the primary sources for term dates.
2. **For the Ottoman-era estate and the family's standing:** consult the Janina (Yanya) vilayet *salnames* and İlkay Erken, *Güney Arnavutluk'ta Üç Osmanlı Kasabası: Avlonya, Berat, Ergiri (19. Yüzyıl)* (Serander, 2021), plus Eqrem bej Vlora's *Kujtime* (which contains a Vrioni genealogy) and the work of historian Ahmet Kondo.
3. **For the communist-era persecution and property:** the AQSH confiscation files cited above and the kujto.al / AIDSSH (Authority for Access to Sigurimi Files) holdings — the latter declassified Jusuf Vrioni's surveillance dossier in 2019 and may hold files on Izedin and other relatives.
4. **Benchmark for revising conclusions:** if a primary document (municipal minutes, an appointment decree, or Guga's book) gives specific term years, update the "single 1940 attestation" finding; if AIDSSH or court records surface an execution/death certificate for Izedin, the "beaten to death 1947" account can be confirmed or corrected.

## Caveats
- **Spelling/identity:** the name appears as Izedin, Izzeddin, Yzedin, Yzenid; the surname as Vrioni/Vërioni/Viryoni. There are at least two same-generation Vrionis who could be confused; this report identifies the Berat mayor as the son of Galip Pasha (brother of Qemal) on the strongest available evidence, while flagging the competing fieri.com genealogy.
- **The "beaten to death in 1947" account** rests on encyclopedic and family/press sources (English Wikipedia; the "Vrioni Family Albania" history reproduced by RADI & RADI and VOAL), not on a located primary document; the date 1947 is consistent across these but unverified by an archival death record.
- **Tension in the record:** the 1946 archive shows leniency on Izedin's property (sequestration lifted, not court-sentenced), while narrative sources report his violent death in 1947; these are chronologically compatible but come from different source types.
- **Byzantine/Palaiologos ancestry** is a traditional claim, not established fact.
- Some dates conflict across sources (e.g., Qemal's death given as 1946, 1950, or 1952; Sami's birth as 1870 or 1876); these conflicts are noted where they occur.
